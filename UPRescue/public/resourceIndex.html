Homeless Shelters: Shelters provide temporary housing and support services for homeless individuals and families. They aim to offer a safe environment for those in need, assisting with basic necessities and the transition to permanent housing.

Addiction Rehabilitation Centers/Programs: These programs offer support and treatment for individuals struggling with substance abuse. Services may include detoxification, therapy, and support groups, designed to help individuals overcome addiction and lead healthier lives.

Alternative Shelter Communities: Alternative shelters offer unique housing solutions outside of traditional shelter systems, such as tiny house villages or communal living spaces. These communities focus on creating supportive environments for homeless individuals or those seeking a more communal lifestyle.

Foodbanks/Meals: Foodbanks and meal programs provide free or low-cost food to individuals and families in need. These resources aim to combat hunger and food insecurity, ensuring that everyone has access to nutritious meals.

Health Clinics: Health clinics offer medical services to the community, often focusing on low-income individuals or those without insurance. Services may include primary care, mental health services, and specialized treatments, aiming to ensure accessible healthcare for all.
